Designed and executed a propensity-score-matched event-study difference-in-differences on a person-month panel of 5.9 million observations (MEPS 2004-2018), with a two-part hurdle decomposition of premium burden and latent class analysis of pre-displacement job profiles.

Found the ACA absorbed roughly one-quarter of the two-month coverage loss and one-third of the four-month loss, with gains concentrated in a high-coverage Finance and Professional class, while three precarious-work classes showed essentially no gains.
